Mobile App Design 2024

Money Budget

Managing your finances can be overwhelming. The Money Budget app is designed to make budgeting simple, rewarding, and stress-free. With intuitive tools for setting budgets, monitoring your spending, and celebrating your savings milestones, Money Budget helps you take control of your financial journey.

Design Process

Step 01

Problem Statement

Many individuals struggle with impulsive spending and lack financial discipline, making it difficult to save money. My subject frequently overspent without a clear system to manage finances, leaving savings goals out of reach. The challenge was to create a solution that transforms spending habits—helping users save consistently while making spending feel like a well-earned reward rather than a source of regret.

Step 02

Background Research

I conducted in-depth interviews to understand daily habits, motivations, and financial challenges. Key insights revealed anxiety around spending, difficulty tracking expenses, and struggles saving for meaningful goals. The subject expressed frustration with impulsive purchases and desired a system that makes saving feel rewarding rather than restrictive. These insights guided the design of a tailored solution.

Step 03

User Interviews

I conducted structured interviews with individuals struggling with budgeting and financial management. Through conversations about their spending habits, saving goals, and challenges, I gained deep insights into their needs and frustrations. The interviews revealed common themes around the desire for simplicity, visual feedback, and positive reinforcement when achieving savings milestones. These findings directly informed the app's core features and user experience design.

Step 04

Competitor Analysis

I analyzed popular budgeting apps like Mint, YNAB, and PocketGuard to identify their features, strengths, and weaknesses. While most offer expense tracking, budgeting categories, and goal setting, common pain points emerged: overwhelming interfaces, limited personalization, and lack of positive reinforcement. This analysis revealed opportunities to design an app that addresses these shortcomings while motivating users to save and spend mindfully.

Step 05

Ideate & Brainstorm

I used the Crazy 8 technique to rapidly sketch user flows and features. This creative exercise helped explore different ways to make budgeting intuitive and engaging. The process identified solutions prioritizing ease of use, clear navigation, and key features like simple goal setting, visual progress tracking, and rewarding saving behaviors.

Step 06

LO-FI Wireframes

Low-fidelity wireframes were created from the Crazy 8 session to quickly test user flows and assess usability. These simple sketches helped visualize the user journey and identify potential pain points early. The wireframes served as a flexible foundation for rapid iteration before moving to high-fidelity designs.

Step 07

User Testing

I conducted usability testing sessions with participants using the low-fidelity wireframes. Users were asked to complete key budgeting tasks such as setting a goal, tracking expenses, and viewing progress. The testing revealed confusion around navigation, unclear information hierarchy, and difficulty understanding the reward system. Based on these findings, I refined the wireframes to improve clarity, simplify flows, and make the savings experience more intuitive before proceeding to high-fidelity designs.

Step 08

High-Fidelity Prototype

After testing and receiving approval, I enhanced the visual design following Apple's Human Interface Guidelines. Building the iOS app in Swift and Xcode was my first experience with these tools. Despite the learning curve, I successfully translated the designs into a high-fidelity prototype, bringing the concept to life.

Conclusion

Reflection

This project was a transformative learning experience, taking me from user-centered design to hands-on app development. By understanding user needs, I crafted thoughtful solutions through research, sketching, and prototyping. Learning Xcode and Swift challenged me to turn concepts into a working product for the first time, strengthening both my design thinking and technical skills. This is my beginning.
