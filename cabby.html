Design Process

Discover

Problem Statement

Many urban commuters face affordability challenges with existing taxi services. There's a need for a cost-effective taxi app solution that ensures accessibility without compromising quality or safety.

Research

Background Research

I analyzed the competitive landscape of transportation apps in Kenya, focusing on Uber, Bolt, and Faras. The research examined strategies, market penetration, user experiences, and service offerings. Key metrics included pricing, availability, reliability, and user satisfaction to understand the dynamic transport app market.

Research

User Interviews

I conducted interviews with frequent ride-hailing app users to understand their transportation needs, preferences, and pain points. Through conversations about their daily commutes, cost concerns, and safety expectations, I gained insights into what matters most to riders. The interviews revealed common themes around affordability, reliability, and the desire for more cost-effective transportation options like carpooling. These findings directly informed the app's feature priorities and user experience design.

Define

Findings

Despite entering the market more recently, Faras emerged as a formidable competitor, overtaking established players like Uber and Bolt. The key to Faras' rapid ascent lies in its consistent offers aimed at enhancing customer satisfaction through strategic promotions and incentives, effectively attracting and retaining a growing user base.

Develop

Feature Priority

Building upon Faras' success strategies, we proposed developing a carpooling section within the app. This feature aims to enhance user experience while promoting cost-saving, comfort, and safety. By facilitating ridesharing among users with common destinations, the app addresses traffic congestion and environmental sustainability while aligning with modern commuter preferences.

Test

User Testing

I conducted usability testing sessions with participants using early prototypes of the carpooling feature. Users were asked to complete key tasks such as booking a shared ride, viewing available carpool options, and understanding the cost savings. The testing revealed navigation challenges, unclear pricing information, and concerns about safety features. Based on these findings, I refined the interface to improve clarity, simplify the booking flow, and address safety concerns before finalizing the design.

Reflection

Reflection

Inspired by Faras' success, our design process led us to develop a business model That shifts pricing to make drivers get 100% of the fare. By addressing customer needs for cost-saving, comfort, and safety, we aim to redefine transportation apps in Kenya. Our commitment to user feedback and excellence ensures we deliver a solution that revolutionizes urban mobility. The idea was not to change the way the app works or looks, but to make the business more affordable for the Drivers and Users.
