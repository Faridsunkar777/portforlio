Luna

Screen time is hard to manage. Luna is an app that helps you manage your screen time and make you more mindful of your time.

Design Process

Discover

Problem Statement

Social media is a time-consuming activity that can be hard to manage. It can be hard to stay focused on your work and goals when you are constantly scrolling through your feed. It can be hard to stay focused on your work and goals when you are constantly scrolling through your feed. A problem that my team and I had in common was that we were all addicted to our phones and we were all spending too much time on social media.

Research

Background Research

We conducted research on the problem of social media addiction and how it affects people's lives. We found that many people are addicted to their phones and are spending too much time on social media. We also found that many people are not aware of the impact of social media on their lives and how it can affect their mental health. Then we realized that there was a need for a solution that would help people manage their time and stay focused on their work and goals.

Research

User Interviews

We conducted in-depth interviews with potential users to understand their screen time habits, pain points, and motivations. Through structured conversations, We valuable insights about their daily routines, struggles with phone addiction, and what would motivate them to change their behavior. The interviews revealed common themes around the need for awareness of time, flexibility, and positive reinforcement rather than strict limitations.

Define

User Persona

We created a user persona based on the user interviews and we found that the user persona is a young adult who is addicted to their phone and is spending too much time on social media. They are not aware of the impact of social media on their lives and how it can affect their mental health. They are looking for a solution that would help them manage their time and stay focused on their work and goals.

Define

User Journey

We created a user journey based on the user persona and we found that the user journey is a young adult who is addicted to their phone and is spending too much time on social media. We found that they when they are using their phone, they aren't aware of the time they spend on their phone. That was a problem that we needed to solve.

Research

Competitor Analysis

My team and I started looking for apps that solve these problems and We found that there are apps that help you manage your time and stay focused on your work and goals. We got to look at apps like Freedom, Screenzen, and opal. All these apps focused on restricting mostly without feeling a sense of control . Most people deleted the apps because it limited them and we figured that we needed to make something that gives them choice rather than fully restricting them completely. That would make our app different. The perfect sweet spot.

Develop

Ideate & Brainstorm

I used the Crazy 8 technique to rapidly sketch user flows and features. This creative exercise helped explore different ways to make the app more engaging and fun. The process identified solutions prioritizing ease of use, clear navigation, and key features like Timer they set for control, Unblocking they can set for unblocking, and a Voice Type to remind them to stay focused and not to use their phone, that way they feel like a choice and a pause to think "Am I really doing this?"

Develop

LO-FI Wireframes

Low-fidelity wireframes were created from the Crazy 8 session to quickly test user flows and assess usability. These simple sketches helped visualize the user journey and identify potential pain points early. The wireframes served as a flexible foundation for rapid iteration before moving to high-fidelity designs.

Test

User Testing

I conducted usability testing sessions with participants using the low-fidelity wireframes. Users were asked to complete key tasks while I observed their interactions and collected feedback. The testing revealed navigation issues, unclear information architecture, and confusion around certain features. Based on these findings, I iterated on the wireframes to improve clarity, simplify flows, and enhance the overall user experience before moving to high-fidelity designs.

Deliver

High-Fidelity Prototype

After testing and receiving approval, I enhanced the visual design following Apple's Human Interface Guidelines. Building the iOS app in Swift and Xcode was my first experience with these tools. Despite the learning curve, I successfully translated the designs into a high-fidelity prototype, bringing the concept to life.

Reflection

Reflection

Reflecting on this project, it truly felt like embarking on a creative journey. I started with a simple question: how can we help people become more mindful of their screen time? Along the way, I experimented with techniques like Crazy 8s, letting my ideas flow freely on paper as I imagined how users might interact with Luna. Through user interviews, I began to understand real stories — the struggles, motivations, and daily routines that shaped people's habits.

Diving into competitor analysis felt like mapping out the landscape and learning what others had built, and what gaps still needed to be filled. Building out personas and mapping user journeys brought the project to life for me, as I could better empathize with our users and see Luna through their eyes. Usability testing sessions then brought valuable feedback, showing where things clicked and where changes were needed to make the app welcoming and intuitive.

Each step, from sketches to high-fidelity prototypes, added a new layer to Luna’s story, teaching me not just about design processes but about the people for whom we design. Ultimately, Luna became more than just a functional tool — it transformed into an experience that was engaging, friendly, and genuinely useful. This journey reminded me that thoughtful design, continuous iteration, and empathy are at the heart of creating products that truly make a difference.
