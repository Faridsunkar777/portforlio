SwiftSekai

Learn Swift and SwiftUI with an engaging macOS app designed for new developers. SwiftSekai offers practical lessons, interactive exercises, and projects, all focused on helping you master Swift at your own pace. Plus, we've built in an AI assistant called Taylor to answer your questions and help you troubleshoot.

Design Process

Step 01

Problem Statement

Self-taught programmers struggle to learn Swift effectively due to fragmented resources and lack of structured guidance. Many learners face challenges finding comprehensive, beginner-friendly materials that combine theory with hands-on practice. The need is for a dedicated macOS platform that offers structured learning paths, interactive exercises, and accessible support to help self-learners master Swift programming confidently.

Step 02

Background Research

I analyzed existing learning platforms and resources for Swift programming, examining their features, teaching methods, and user experiences. The research focused on understanding the learning needs of self-taught programmers and identifying gaps in current educational resources.

Step 03

User Interviews

I conducted in-depth interviews with self-taught Swift programmers to understand their learning journey, challenges, and needs. Through conversations about their experiences with existing resources, struggles with complex concepts, and desire for structured guidance, I gained insights into what would make learning Swift more effective. The interviews revealed common themes around the need for progressive learning paths, interactive practice, and immediate help when stuck. These findings directly informed the platform's core features and AI assistant design.

Step 04

Findings

Research revealed that self-taught programmers need structured learning paths, interactive exercises, and immediate feedback. Many existing resources lack personalization and fail to provide hands-on practice opportunities. The findings highlighted the importance of an AI assistant that can answer questions and provide guidance in real-time.

Step 05

LO-FI Wireframes

Low-fidelity wireframes were created to visualize the user journey and test different interface layouts. These wireframes helped define the structure of lessons, exercises, and the AI assistant interface.

Step 06

User Testing

I conducted usability testing sessions with self-taught programmers using the low-fidelity wireframes. Users were asked to navigate through lessons, complete exercises, and interact with the AI assistant. The testing revealed confusion around lesson progression, unclear exercise instructions, and difficulty accessing help when needed. Based on these findings, I refined the wireframes to improve navigation clarity, simplify the learning flow, and make the AI assistant more accessible before moving to high-fidelity designs.

Conclusion

Reflection

SwiftSekai represents a comprehensive solution for self-taught Swift programmers. By combining structured learning paths, interactive exercises, and an AI assistant named Taylor, the platform addresses the key challenges faced by self-learners. This project demonstrates the importance of understanding user needs and creating tailored solutions that empower learners to achieve their goals.
